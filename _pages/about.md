I am an Assistant Professor of Information Security and Assurance in the Information Systems Department, located in the Michael J. Coles College of Business at Kennesaw State University in Kennesaw, Georgia. I currently serve as the program coordinator for the Information Security and Assurance undergraduate degree program, as well as the faculty advisor for the award-winning student-led KSU Offensive Security Research Club.

I have almost two decades of experience in information security. Before entering academia full-time, I worked as an information security consultant, focusing primarily on the needs of small and medium-sized businesses. Prior to that, I worked in the healthcare IT field, where I developed and supported transcription interfaces for medical facilities throughout the United States.

My research interests focus on problems found at the intersection of security, privacy, and public policy, and my work has been published in top-tier academic journals. I am also a co-author of several academic textbooks on various information security-related topics that have been used in classrooms globally. In addition, I frequently contribute to local, regional, and national media outlets on issues related to information security.
